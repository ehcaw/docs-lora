### The Millennium Falcon

The YT-1300 light freighter piloted by Han Solo and Chewbacca is arguably the most famous ship in Star Wars. Despite its worn appearance, the Falcon made the Kessel Run in less than twelve parsecs.

<Info>
The Millennium Falcon has been modified extensively, making it faster than ships many times its size.
</Info>

## Imperial ships

The Galactic Empire relies on standardized, mass-produced vessels:

- **TIE Fighter** - Fast, maneuverable, but lacks shields
- **TIE Interceptor** - Enhanced speed and firepower
- **TIE Bomber** - Designed for surface assault
- **Star Destroyer** - Massive wedge-shaped capital ships

### Star Destroyers

Imperial Star Destroyers serve as the backbone of the Imperial Navy. At 1,600 meters long, these vessels carry thousands of troops, dozens of TIE fighters, and enough firepower to devastate a planet's surface.

<Note>
The Super Star Destroyer *Executor* measured 19 kilometers in length and served as Darth Vader's flagship.
</Note>

## Prequel era ships

The prequel trilogy introduced distinctive ship designs:

1. **Naboo N-1 Starfighter** - Elegant yellow fighters from Naboo
2. **Jedi Starfighter** - Compact interceptors used by Jedi Knights
3. **Republic Gunship** - Troop transport used in the Clone Wars
4. **Venator-class Star Destroyer** - Predecessor to the Imperial Star Destroyer

## Sequel era ships

The sequel trilogy featured updated versions of classic designs:

- **T-70 X-wing** - Modernized Resistance fighter
- **First Order TIE Fighter** - Enhanced with shields
- **Kylo Ren's TIE Silencer** - Prototype advanced fighter

## Legacy

The starships of Star Wars have become cultural icons, inspiring generations of fans and influencing spacecraft design in other media. From model kits to video games, these vessels continue to capture imaginations across the galaxy.
