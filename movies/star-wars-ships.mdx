## Rebel Alliance ships

### X-wing starfighter

The T-65B X-wing became the symbol of the Rebel Alliance, known for destroying both Death Stars.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Manufacturer** | Incom Corporation |
| **Length** | 12.5 meters |
| **Armament** | 4 laser cannons, proton torpedoes |
| **Hyperdrive** | Yes |

### A-wing starfighter

The RZ-1 A-wing interceptor was the fastest starfighter in the Rebel fleet, used for hit-and-run attacks.

### Y-wing starfighter

An older but reliable bomber used throughout the Galactic Civil War.

## Imperial ships

### TIE Fighter

The Twin Ion Engine fighter served as the Empire's primary starfighter, produced in massive quantities.

<Info>
TIE fighters lack shields and hyperdrives, making them cheap to produce but dangerous to pilot.
</Info>

### TIE Interceptor

A faster, more maneuverable variant with enhanced firepower.

## Freighters and transports

### Millennium Falcon

Han Solo's modified YT-1300 light freighter is perhaps the most famous ship in the galaxy.

> "She may not look like much, but she's got it where it counts." — Han Solo

**Notable features:**
- Modified hyperdrive (0.5 past lightspeed)
- Hidden smuggling compartments
- Quad laser cannons
- Deflector shields

### Ghost

The VCX-100 light freighter served as home base for the crew of *Star Wars Rebels*.

### Death Star

While technically a space station, the Death Star deserves mention as the Empire's ultimate weapon.

- **Death Star I** - Destroyed at the Battle of Yavin
- **Death Star II** - Destroyed at the Battle of Endor

## Unique vehicles

- **Razor Crest** - Din Djarin's gunship from *The Mandalorian*
- **Ebon Hawk** - Legendary ship from the Old Republic era
- **Tantive IV** - Princess Leia's diplomatic corvette
- **Naboo Royal Starship** - Elegant chrome vessel from Naboo

## Ground vehicles

### AT-AT

The All Terrain Armored Transport became an icon of Imperial military might during the Battle of Hoth.

### Speeder bikes

Used by both Imperial scouts and Rebel forces for rapid reconnaissance.

## Legacy

Star Wars spacecraft designs have influenced real-world aerospace concepts and remain among the most recognizable vehicles in science fiction. From the distinctive howl of a TIE fighter to the saucer shape of the Millennium Falcon, these ships are integral to the saga's enduring appeal.
