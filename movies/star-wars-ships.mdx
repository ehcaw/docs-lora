### X-wing starfighter

The T-65B X-wing is the primary starfighter of the Rebel Alliance and later the Resistance. Known for its distinctive S-foils that lock into attack position.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Manufacturer** | Incom Corporation |
| **Length** | 12.5 meters |
| **Armament** | 4 laser cannons, proton torpedoes |
| **Notable pilots** | Luke Skywalker, Poe Dameron |

### TIE Fighter

The Twin Ion Engine fighter serves as the Empire's primary starfighter. Fast and maneuverable but lacks shields and hyperdrive.

### Millennium Falcon

> "She may not look like much, but she's got it where it counts." — Han Solo

The Corellian YT-1300 freighter is the fastest ship in the galaxy, famously completing the Kessel Run in less than twelve parsecs.

### AT-AT Walker

The All Terrain Armored Transport is the Empire's fearsome ground assault vehicle. Standing 22 meters tall, these walkers terrorized Rebel forces at the Battle of Hoth.

### AT-ST Walker

The smaller "chicken walker" provides reconnaissance and infantry support, famously defeated by Ewoks on Endor.

## Podracers

High-speed racing vehicles popular on Outer Rim worlds like Tatooine. Anakin Skywalker won his freedom in a podrace at age nine.

## The Death Star

While technically a space station, the Death Star represents the ultimate Imperial weapon—a moon-sized battle station capable of destroying entire planets.

<Warning>
The Empire constructed two Death Stars before the Emperor's defeat at the Battle of Endor.
</Warning>

## Legacy

Star Wars vehicles have inspired generations of fans and influenced countless science fiction designs. From toys to video games, these iconic ships remain central to the franchise's enduring appeal.
