## Desert planets

### Tatooine

The iconic twin-suns desert world where both Anakin and Luke Skywalker grew up.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Suns** | Two (Tatoo I and Tatoo II) |
| **Notable locations** | Mos Eisley, Lars Homestead, Jabba's Palace |
| **Native species** | Jawas, Tusken Raiders |

### Jakku

A desolate junkyard planet where Rey was abandoned as a child.

## Ice and snow worlds

- **Hoth** - Rebel Alliance base featured in *The Empire Strikes Back*
- **Ilum** - Sacred Jedi world where kyber crystals are harvested

## Forest and jungle worlds

<Info>
Endor's forest moon is home to the Ewoks who helped defeat the Empire.
</Info>

- **Endor** - Forest moon where the second Death Star was destroyed
- **Kashyyyk** - Wookiee homeworld covered in massive wroshyr trees
- **Dagobah** - Swamp planet where Yoda lived in exile

## Volcanic and industrial worlds

- **Mustafar** - Volcanic world where Anakin and Obi-Wan dueled
- **Geonosis** - Rocky planet where the Clone Wars began

## Ocean and tropical worlds

- **Kamino** - Ocean planet where the clone army was created
- **Scarif** - Tropical world housing Imperial archives
- **Ahch-To** - Remote oceanic planet where Luke exiled himself

## City planets

### Bespin

A gas giant with Cloud City, a floating mining colony run by Lando Calrissian.

> "I've just made a deal that will keep the Empire out of here forever." — Lando Calrissian

## Significance in the saga

Each planet in Star Wars serves both as a backdrop for adventure and a reflection of the story's themes. From the harsh deserts that forge heroes to the corrupt cityscapes of power, these worlds shape the characters who inhabit them.
