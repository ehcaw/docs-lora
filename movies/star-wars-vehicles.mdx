### X-wing

The T-65B X-wing starfighter became the symbol of the Rebel Alliance. Its four laser cannons and proton torpedoes made it effective against both fighters and capital ships.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Manufacturer** | Incom Corporation |
| **Length** | 12.5 meters |
| **Crew** | 1 pilot, 1 astromech droid |
| **Weapons** | 4 laser cannons, proton torpedoes |

### TIE Fighter

The Twin Ion Engine (TIE) fighter served as the Empire's standard starfighter. Fast and maneuverable but lacking shields and hyperdrives.

<Info>
TIE fighters were designed to be deployed in overwhelming numbers, relying on quantity over individual pilot survivability.
</Info>

## Capital ships

### Star Destroyer

The Imperial Star Destroyer represents Imperial military might. These massive wedge-shaped vessels served as mobile bases for planetary operations.

- **Imperial-class** - 1,600 meters long, carried 72 TIE fighters
- **Super Star Destroyer** - Over 19,000 meters long, Darth Vader's flagship

### Mon Calamari cruisers

The Rebel Alliance's primary capital ships, converted from civilian vessels by the Mon Calamari people.

### AT-AT walkers

The imposing four-legged walkers became symbols of Imperial ground forces. Featured prominently in the Battle of Hoth.

### Landspeeder

Luke Skywalker's X-34 landspeeder was his primary transportation on Tatooine before joining the Rebellion.

## The Death Star

<Warning>
The Death Star, while technically a space station, functioned as a mobile weapons platform capable of destroying entire planets.
</Warning>

Two Death Stars were constructed:
- **Death Star I** - Destroyed at the Battle of Yavin
- **Death Star II** - Destroyed at the Battle of Endor
