# Tom and Jerry

**Tom and Jerry** is an American animated media franchise and series of comedy short films created in 1940 by William Hanna and Joseph Barbera. It centers on the rivalry between the titular characters—a cat named Tom and a mouse named Jerry.

## Overview

| Detail | Information |

| **First short** | February 10, 1940 |
| **Studio** | Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer (originally) |
| **Total shorts** | 164 theatrical shorts (1940-1967) |
| **Academy Awards** | 7 wins |

### Tom

Tom is a gray and white tuxedo cat who lives a comfortable life in a suburban home. His main goal is to catch Jerry, though his schemes almost always backfire spectacularly. Despite their rivalry, Tom occasionally shows a softer side toward Jerry.

### Jerry

Jerry is a clever, resourceful brown mouse who lives in the same house as Tom. He consistently outsmarts Tom using his intelligence, speed, and the environment around him. Jerry often wins their encounters but sometimes teams up with Tom against common enemies.

## Supporting characters

- **Spike (Butch)** - A gray bulldog who often gets caught in Tom and Jerry's antics
- **Tyke** - Spike's son, a small puppy
- **Nibbles (Tuffy)** - A small gray mouse and Jerry's nephew
- **Mammy Two Shoes** - Tom's owner in early cartoons (shown only from the waist down)

## Classic formula

The typical Tom and Jerry short follows a simple but effective pattern:

1. Tom spots Jerry and decides to catch him
2. Jerry outsmarts Tom with clever tricks
3. Tom's elaborate plans backfire comedically
4. The chase escalates with increasingly absurd situations
5. Jerry emerges victorious (usually)

<Info>
Tom and Jerry cartoons are largely silent, relying on visual comedy and musical scores rather than dialogue.
</Info>

## Academy Award success

Tom and Jerry won seven Academy Awards for Best Animated Short Film:

1. *The Milky Waif* (1946)
2. *The Cat Concerto* (1947)
3. *The Little Orphan* (1949)
4. *The Two Mouseketeers* (1952)
5. *Johann Mouse* (1953)
6. *Touché, Pussy Cat!* (1954)
7. *Good Will to Men* (1955 - remake)

## Cultural impact

Tom and Jerry has influenced animation for over 80 years:

- Pioneered slapstick comedy in animation
- Set standards for cartoon violence and comedic timing
- Inspired countless other animated series
- Remains popular through TV broadcasts, streaming, and merchandise

## Legacy

> "Don't you believe it!"

Tom and Jerry continues to entertain audiences worldwide, with new series, films, and crossovers keeping the franchise alive. The simple premise of a cat chasing a mouse has proven timeless, making Tom and Jerry one of the most beloved animated franchises in history.
