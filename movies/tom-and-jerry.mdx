# Tom and Jerry

**Tom and Jerry** is an American animated media franchise featuring the comedic rivalry between Tom, a cat, and Jerry, a mouse. Created by William Hanna and Joseph Barbera, the series debuted in 1940 and has become one of the most successful and beloved animated properties in entertainment history.

- **Spike (Butch)** - A grey bulldog who often protects Jerry from Tom
- **Tyke** - Spike's son, a small puppy
- **Nibbles (Tuffy)** - A small grey mouse, Jerry's nephew
- **Mammy Two Shoes** - Tom's owner (original shorts)

## History

### The golden era (1940-1958)

The original Tom and Jerry shorts were produced by MGM's cartoon studio. Hanna and Barbera directed 114 shorts, winning seven Academy Awards for Best Animated Short Film:

1. *The Yankee Doodle Mouse* (1943)
2. *Mouse Trouble* (1944)
3. *Quiet Please!* (1945)
4. *The Cat Concerto* (1947)
5. *The Little Orphan* (1949)
6. *The Two Mouseketeers* (1952)
7. *Johann Mouse* (1953)

### Later productions

After the MGM studio closed in 1958, Tom and Jerry continued through various productions:

- **Gene Deitch era** (1961-1962) - 13 shorts produced in Prague
- **Chuck Jones era** (1963-1967) - 34 shorts with stylistic changes
- **Filmation series** (1975-1977) - Saturday morning cartoon
- **Tom and Jerry Kids** (1990-1994) - Young versions of the characters
- **Tom and Jerry Tales** (2006-2008) - Modern theatrical-style shorts

## Legacy

Tom and Jerry's influence on animation and popular culture is immeasurable:

- One of the most-watched cartoons worldwide
- Inspired countless chase-comedy cartoons
- Cultural references in films, music, and art
- Merchandise and theme park attractions globally

<Info>
Tom and Jerry holds the record for the most Academy Awards won by an animated theatrical series, with seven wins out of thirteen nominations.
</Info>

## Memorable elements

### Signature gags

The series is known for its slapstick humor and creative visual comedy:

- Elaborate traps and contraptions
- Exaggerated physical reactions
- Fourth-wall breaking moments
- Musical integration and choreography

### The music

The original shorts featured meticulously timed musical scores by Scott Bradley, who composed original music synchronized to the on-screen action, elevating the comedy to an art form.

> "Tom and Jerry is not just a cartoon. It's a masterclass in visual storytelling, timing, and comedy that transcends language and culture."
