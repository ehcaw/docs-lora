# Tom and Jerry

**Tom and Jerry** is an American animated media franchise and series of comedy short films created in 1940 by William Hanna and Joseph Barbera. It centers on the rivalry between Tom the cat and Jerry the mouse.

## Overview

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Created by** | William Hanna and Joseph Barbera |
| **First appearance** | February 10, 1940 |
| **Studio** | Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer (originally) |
| **Total shorts** | 164 theatrical shorts (1940-1967) |
| **Academy Awards** | 7 wins |

## Main characters

- **Tom** - A gray and white domestic shorthair cat who endlessly pursues Jerry
- **Jerry** - A small brown house mouse who outwits Tom at every turn
- **Spike** - A gray bulldog who often protects Jerry from Tom
- **Tyke** - Spike's son, a small puppy
- **Nibbles/Tuffy** - A small gray mouse and Jerry's nephew

## The classic formula

The series follows a simple but effective premise: Tom tries to catch Jerry, and Jerry outsmarts him. Despite the constant chase, the two characters occasionally team up against a common enemy or show genuine care for each other.

<Info>
Tom and Jerry won seven Academy Awards for Best Animated Short Film, more than any other animated series.
</Info>

## Cultural impact

Tom and Jerry became one of the most beloved and recognizable animated properties worldwide. The series is known for:

- Slapstick comedy and visual gags
- Minimal dialogue relying on physical humor
- Classical music integration
- Influence on animation techniques
- Universal appeal across cultures and generations

## Notable shorts

1. *The Midnight Snack* (1941) - First official Tom and Jerry cartoon
2. *Yankee Doodle Mouse* (1943) - Academy Award winner
3. *Mouse Trouble* (1944) - Academy Award winner
4. *The Cat Concerto* (1947) - Academy Award winner featuring Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2
5. *Johann Mouse* (1953) - Academy Award winner

## Legacy

> "Is there anybody who hasn't seen Tom and Jerry?"

The franchise continues to thrive with new animated series, films, and merchandise. Tom and Jerry remain cultural icons, representing the golden age of American animation while entertaining new generations of viewers.
