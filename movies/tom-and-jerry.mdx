# Tom and Jerry

**Tom and Jerry** is an American animated media franchise and series of comedy short films created in 1940 by William Hanna and Joseph Barbera. The series features a rivalry between Tom, a cat, and Jerry, a mouse.

## Overview

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Created by** | William Hanna, Joseph Barbera |
| **First appearance** | February 10, 1940 |
| **Studio** | Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer (original) |
| **Total shorts** | 164 theatrical shorts (1940-1967) |
| **Academy Awards** | 7 wins |

## Main characters

- **Tom Cat** - A gray and white domestic shorthair cat who persistently chases Jerry
- **Jerry Mouse** - A small brown house mouse who consistently outsmarts Tom
- **Spike** - A gray bulldog who often gets caught in the crossfire
- **Tyke** - Spike's son, a small puppy
- **Nibbles/Tuffy** - A small gray mouse, Jerry's nephew

## What makes it special

The original theatrical shorts are notable for their:

1. **Minimal dialogue** - The comedy relies almost entirely on visual gags and physical humor
2. **Classical music** - Orchestral scores enhance the comedic timing
3. **Slapstick violence** - Exaggerated, cartoon violence that became a genre staple
4. **Universal appeal** - No language barrier makes it enjoyable worldwide

<Info>
Tom and Jerry won seven Academy Awards for Best Animated Short Film, more than any other animated series.
</Info>

## Cultural impact

Tom and Jerry has influenced animation for over eight decades:

- Pioneered chase-based cartoon comedy
- Inspired countless imitators and homages
- Remains popular through streaming and merchandise
- Continues with new productions and a 2021 live-action/animated film

## Legacy

> "Is there anything funnier than a cat chasing a mouse?"

The simple premise of a cat-and-mouse chase has proven timeless. Despite the constant conflict, Tom and Jerry occasionally show moments of friendship, revealing a complex relationship beneath the rivalry.
