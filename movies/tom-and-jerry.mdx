# Tom and Jerry

**Tom and Jerry** is an American animated media franchise featuring the slapstick comedy rivalry between Tom, a gray and white domestic cat, and Jerry, a small brown mouse. Created by William Hanna and Joseph Barbera, the series became one of the most beloved animated properties in entertainment history.

### Tom Cat

Tom (originally "Jasper") is a gray and white tuxedo cat who lives a comfortable life as a house cat. His primary goal is catching Jerry, though he rarely succeeds. Despite their rivalry, Tom and Jerry occasionally work together against common enemies.

### Jerry Mouse

Jerry is a small brown mouse who lives in a hole in the wall of the house where Tom resides. He is clever, resourceful, and almost always manages to outwit Tom, often turning Tom's own traps against him.

### Supporting characters

- **Spike** - A gray bulldog who often protects Jerry
- **Tyke** - Spike's son, a small puppy
- **Nibbles/Tuffy** - A gray mouse who is Jerry's nephew
- **Mammy Two Shoes** - Tom's owner in early shorts

## Theatrical shorts era

The original theatrical shorts produced by MGM are divided into several periods:

1. *Hanna-Barbera era* (1940-1958) - 114 shorts
2. *Gene Deitch era* (1961-1962) - 13 shorts
3. *Chuck Jones era* (1963-1967) - 34 shorts

<Info>
The series won seven Academy Awards for Best Animated Short Film, more than any other theatrical animated series.
</Info>

## Award-winning shorts

The Oscar-winning Tom and Jerry shorts include:

- *The Yankee Doodle Mouse* (1943)
- *Mouse Trouble* (1944)
- *Quiet Please!* (1945)
- *The Cat Concerto* (1947)
- *The Little Orphan* (1948)
- *The Two Mouseketeers* (1952)
- *Johann Mouse* (1953)

## Signature elements

The series is known for:

- **Slapstick comedy** - Physical humor with exaggerated violence
- **Musical scores** - Classical music synchronized with action
- **Minimal dialogue** - Visual storytelling with little to no speech
- **Chase sequences** - Creative and elaborate pursuit scenarios
- **Household settings** - Most action takes place in domestic environments

## Cultural impact

> "Is there anything funnier than a cat chasing a mouse?"

Tom and Jerry influenced generations of animators and remains a cultural touchstone for physical comedy in animation. The series has been continuously in production in various forms since 1940.

The franchise includes:

- Theatrical shorts (1940-1967)
- Television series (multiple iterations)
- Direct-to-video films
- Theatrical feature films
- Video games and merchandise

## Legacy

Tom and Jerry's simple premise of cat-versus-mouse has entertained audiences for over eight decades. The characters continue to appear in new productions while the classic shorts remain beloved worldwide, demonstrating the timeless appeal of well-crafted animated comedy.
