# The Avengers

**The Avengers** (2012) is a superhero film produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. Directed by Joss Whedon, it brought together Marvel's greatest heroes for the first time on the big screen, forever changing the landscape of superhero cinema.

## Plot summary

When the Asgardian god Loki arrives on Earth with a plan to enslave humanity using the Tesseract (an infinity stone of immense power), Nick Fury, director of S.H.I.E.L.D., activates the Avengers Initiative. He assembles a team of extraordinary individuals:

- **Iron Man (Tony Stark)** - Genius billionaire in a powered suit of armor
- **Captain America (Steve Rogers)** - Super-soldier from World War II
- **Thor** - Asgardian god of thunder and Loki's brother
- **Hulk (Bruce Banner)** - Scientist who transforms into a green rage monster
- **Black Widow (Natasha Romanoff)** - Expert spy and assassin
- **Hawkeye (Clint Barton)** - Master archer

The heroes must overcome their differences and learn to work as a team to stop Loki and his Chitauri army from conquering Earth.

## Production

### Development

The Avengers was the culmination of Marvel Studios' ambitious plan to create a shared cinematic universe. The groundwork was laid through five preceding films:

1. *Iron Man* (2008)
2. *The Incredible Hulk* (2008)
3. *Iron Man 2* (2010)
4. *Thor* (2011)
5. *Captain America: The First Avenger* (2011)

### Filming

Principal photography took place from April to September 2011, primarily in Albuquerque, New Mexico, and Cleveland, Ohio. The iconic Battle of New York sequence was filmed on location in Manhattan.

## Reception

### Critical response

The film received widespread critical acclaim for its direction, screenplay, action sequences, visual effects, and performances. Critics praised Whedon's ability to balance multiple characters while delivering an entertaining and coherent story.

### Box office success

The Avengers broke numerous box office records upon its release:

- Highest-grossing opening weekend at the time ($207.4 million domestic)
- First film to earn over $200 million in a single weekend
- Third highest-grossing film of all time (at time of release)

## Legacy

The Avengers proved that a shared universe model could work in cinema, paving the way for:

- Multiple MCU phases and over 30 interconnected films
- Other studios attempting shared universes
- A new era of blockbuster filmmaking
